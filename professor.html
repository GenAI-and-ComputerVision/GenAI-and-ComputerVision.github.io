Chaoning Zhang

Assistant Professor

About Professor

Education and Work

Chaoning Zhang (Senior Member, IEEE) received the B.S. and master's degrees in Electrical Engineering from the Harbin Institute of Technology, Harbin, China, in 2012 and 2015, respectively (with a second master's degree in Engineering and Policy Analysis from TU Delft, Delft, The Netherlands), and the Ph.D. degree in Electrical Engineering from Korea Advanced Institute of Science Technology (KAIST), South Korea, in 2021. He worked at LS Electric, Korea, for 2.5 years (from August 2015 to February 2018) and did one-year post-doctoral research at KAIST (from September 2021 to August 2022). Since September 2022, he has been an Assistant Professor at Kyung Hee University (Department of Artificial Intelligence, School of Computing).

Research and Publication

His research interests include computer vision and generative AI. He has co-authored more than 100 papers and notably, he published more than 10 first-author top-conference papers, including 3 CVPR, 2 ICCV, 2 AAAI, 2 IJCAI, 2 ACM MM, 1 ECCV, 1 NeurIPS, 1 ICLR. He also has multiple first-author works published at WACV and BMVC (second-tier conferences in computer vision). His current focus is to help the students publish their first-author papers in top conferences and journals. Multiple works have been accepted (like TMLR, PR, IEEE IoT, TMM) and more are currently under review.

Awards and Languages

He is a recipient of Qualcomm Innovation Fellowship (2020, Korea) and Outstanding Paper Award at CVPR Workshop on AML-CV (2021). He has been invited to do research and seminar talks at Tsinghua University, KAIST, Postech, UNIST, etc. He has learned multiple languages: Chinese (Native), English (IELTS 8), Korean (TOPIK Level 6), and Japanese (JLPT N1). He won 2nd place in KAIST Korean Speech Competition Award in 2018. He supervises the students in English, Korean and Chinese.
